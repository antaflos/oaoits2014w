Business Continuity Management

A management process covering Disaster Recovery Planning and Business Continuity Planning to ensure critical business functions can continue during and after major incidents, with minimal losses of operations, systems and life.

Business Continuity Management

  • Maximum Tolerable Downtime (MTD): Amount of time critical business functions may be out of service after a disaster
  • Recovery Point Objective (RPO): Amount of acceptable data, measured in time, that can be lost from a disaster
  • Recovery Time Objective (RTO): Amount of time it may take to restore business functions after a disaster
  • Work Recovery Time (WRT): Amount of time it may take to recover data and bring infrastructure back so that normal operation can continue

Business Impact Analysis

  • MTD, RPO, RTO and WRT are the result of a Business Impact Analysis (BIA)
  • BIA identifies business functions, activities and resources, and their criticality levels
  • BIA identifies threats and vulnerabilities to these functions, and calculates the risks for each

Backup Requirements

  • RPO dictates the backup frequency
  • RTO/WRT dictate the kind of backup and restore technology or technique to use

Risks - Availability

  • The backup solution should be autonomous from the main system
  • The backup solution should be easy to access

Risks - Integrity

  • The backup solution should constantly create backups of the system
  • The backup solution should guaranty the integrity of the data
  • The backup solution should also contain a version control system

Risks - Confidentiality

  • The backup solution should have built-in security features:
    • Encryption of the data
    • User access control

Hardware and software backup solutions

  • Pros: Security, Access, Knowledge of the functionality
  • Cons: Complexity, Cost (Hardware), Adaptability, Scalability
    • IBM Tivoli Storage Manager: US$ 100-200 per Processor Value Unit

Tools

Cloud backup solutions

  • Pros: Distributed solution, Complexity, Cost
  • Cons: Security, Access, Knowledge of the functionality
    • Amazon S3: US$ 15 per gigabyte and month

Tools

Deduplication backup solutions

  • Pros: Data size
  • Cons: Integrity, Performance
    • Dell DR6000: US$ 60.000 for 9TB of data
    • EMC Data Domain: US$ 45.000 for 133TB of data
